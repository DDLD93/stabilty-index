# Nigeria Stability Index — Version 2 (proposed)

This document describes **what we want Version 2 to deliver**, in everyday language. It is meant for partners, funders, and teams who need the direction of travel—not technical specifications.

---

## Why Version 2

Version 2 is about **closing the loop**: from collecting how people feel about Nigeria each month, to **staying in touch**, **surfacing past results openly**, **understanding how the site is used**, **being easy to find online**, and **enriching the story of the five pillars** with trusted external signals.

---

## 1. Subscribe and newsletter — everywhere it matters

**Today:** There is a dedicated subscribe experience and clear copy about monthly updates, but signing up does not yet plug into a full email program across the whole site.

**Version 2:** We will **fully implement** “Subscribe now” and the **newsletter** so that:

- **Monthly cycle pages** (each survey period) invite people to join the list in context—right where they are reading about that month’s check-in or results.
- **Other natural spots** across the public site do the same: the home journey, report pages, after someone finishes the survey (optional, respectful prompt), and the contact or updates areas—always with simple language and a clear privacy promise.

**What success looks like:** A visitor can join once, manage preferences or unsubscribe easily, and receive **reliable** monthly notes when new material is ready—without hunting for a hidden form.

---

## 2. Public library of all published cycles

**Today:** Published outputs are tied to an editorial workflow; browsing “everything we have ever published” as a visitor is not the main experience.

**Version 2:** Anyone can **browse a complete, public list** of every **published** survey cycle and its **published index report** (the snapshot the team releases). Think of it as a **reading room** or **archive**: sortable or filterable by time, with plain titles so people instantly know which month or period they are opening.

**What success looks like:** Journalists, researchers, and curious citizens can **scroll or search the archive**, open any published period, and compare how the narrative and scores evolved—without signing in.

---

## 3. Full picture of how people use the site

**Version 2:** We will treat **analytics** as a core product capability: not to identify individuals in the survey, but to **understand patterns**—what people open, where they drop off, which reports get shared, and which paths lead to a completed check-in.

**What success looks like:** Product and editorial teams can answer questions like “Are people finding last month’s report?” and “Where do we lose people on mobile?” using **consistent, privacy-conscious** reporting. Decisions about layout, copy, and timing of cycles are backed by **observed behaviour**, not guesswork.

*(Survey responses themselves remain governed by your existing privacy commitments; analytics focuses on **site interaction** unless you explicitly choose to join those worlds in a documented way.)*

---

## 4. Search engines and AI-friendly discovery

**Version 2:** The public face of the index will be **optimized for discovery**:

- **Search engines** can crawl and understand each important page (titles, descriptions, structured summaries where helpful) so that searches for “Nigeria stability index,” a given month, or a pillar name are more likely to surface **your** official pages.
- **AI assistants and answer engines** get clearer signals from the site (well-labeled sections, consistent wording for the five pillars, and summaries that machines can quote accurately).

**What success looks like:** More people arrive from Google or from an AI summary **with correct context**—and land on the right report or explainer, not a scraper or outdated copy.

---

## 5. Feelings layer from a remote partner API

The index is built around **five pillars** of stability (for example: security, economy and currency, investor confidence, governance, and social cohesion—the exact names stay aligned with your live survey).

**Version 2:** We will **integrate a remote service** (API) that supplies an additional **“how people are feeling”** signal **mapped to those same five pillars**. That might be sentiment, stress indicators, or another agreed metric—delivered in a structured way so the site can show it **beside** or **within** the official survey story, with clear labeling so users know **what comes from the index** and **what comes from the partner feed**.

**What success looks like:** Readers see a **richer, still honest** picture: your crowdsourced check-in plus a **consistent, automated feelings layer** that updates on a schedule you agree with the provider—without manual retyping.

Testing, handover, and light documentation sit **inside** each workstream’s figure rather than as a separate row. Actual spend may shift slightly between rows as the build progresses, without changing the total envelope unless you agree a scope change.


| Workstream                    | What this covers (plain language)                                                                                                                     | Amount (₦)    |

| **Public archive**            | The “reading room”: list and detail experience for every published cycle/report, navigation, and mobile-friendly layout                               | 210,000       |

**Not included in the figure above:** ongoing costs after launch (for example, email volume charges, or per-call fees from the feelings API provider). Those are **operational** and depend on audience size and contract terms.

## Closing note

Version 2 does not change **why** the Nigeria Stability Index exists—it strengthens **reach**, **transparency**, **learning from usage**, and **depth of insight** so the monthly national check-in remains credible and useful as the audience grows.
